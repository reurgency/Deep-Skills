Video production guide

This curriculum is built to be narrated. Here's how to turn the six pages into a video series — a recurring structure, suggested runtimes, the on-screen visuals each segment needs, and the narration beats that carry each episode.

The thesis

Teach the “why,” demonstrate the “how”

The hard part of these skills isn't the commands — it's the judgment: when to drill vs break out, when a finding is real, why a green test can lie. Each episode should spend its first third on the idea (a recurring principle made concrete), its middle on a live screen-capture run, and its last third on the design decisions that explain why the skill nudges you the way it does.

The one-line hook for the whole series

“Four AI agents that each do exactly one job — and refuse to do the others — turn a feature from idea into verified code, where any one of them could be picked up cold by a stranger.”

Repeatable

A five-beat structure for every skill episode

  1. Cold open — the failure it prevents (0:00–0:45)

    Open on the bug this skill exists to catch. Show the symptom first (the button that double-fires, the green test that lied), then name the skill as the fix.

  2. The mental model (0:45–2:30)

    State which of the four recurring principles this episode makes concrete. Use the panel quote from the skill page verbatim — it's written to be read aloud.

  3. Live run (2:30–7:00)

    Screen-capture the skill on a small real feature. Narrate the decisions, not the keystrokes. Pause on each artifact as it's written.

  4. Why it's built this way (7:00–10:00)

    Pull 3–4 of the design decisions from the skill page. For each: the choice, the rejected alternative, the reason. This is the segment that separates a tutorial from a course.

  5. Mistakes & the checklist (10:00–11:30)

    Run the “common mistakes” as quick on-screen cards, then the mastery checklist as the takeaway. End on the hand-off to the next skill.

Keep it consistent

A recurring visual language

COLOR PER SKILL

One hue per episode

Plan = blue, Plan-review = purple, Implement = green, Code-review = amber, Roadmap = pink. The HTML pages already use these — match lower-thirds and title cards to them.

THE RELAY

The pipeline bar, always on screen

Keep the four-stage relay (produce → critique → execute → verify) as a persistent header strip, highlighting the current stage. It orients the viewer in the loop at all times.

THE ARTIFACT TREE

Show where output lands

Every time a skill writes a file, cut to the .deep-skills/<effort>/ tree and highlight the new file. Reinforces that the artifacts are the product.

EVIDENCE CALLOUTS

Cite on screen

When the narration claims something, flash the path:line or the rule it comes from. The series preaches evidence — the video should practice it.

The shot list

Episode-by-episode plan

Episode 0 — Series overview · ~8 min

Hook + the one loop Four principles Artifact tree Deep-Learn teaser

Goal: sell the whole system before any one skill. Visuals: the relay diagram animating left-to-right; the four-principle cards; the artifact tree filling in stage by stage. Narration beat: end on the capstone test — “a teammate can open only your .deep-skills/ directory and reconstruct everything.” Source page: Overview.

Episode 1 — deep-plan · ~11 min · blue

Cold open: a plan that needs the chat to make sense Live: framing → explore → questions → contract

Money shot: producing just the restatement + three non-goals + effort name, then stopping — the discipline of the most-skipped step. Decisions to feature: planning-only boundary, /drill vs /breakout, the Data-Flow Contract (show a row you can't fill without finding the bug). Source: Episode 1.

Episode 2 — deep-plan-review · ~9 min · purple

Cold open: the planner can't see its own blind spot Live: brief fresh agents on two lenses

Money shot: the briefing — show exactly what goes in (plan + lenses + preference sources) and that the planning transcript is deliberately left out. Decisions: independence/withhold-transcript, two lenses, multi-agent fan-out + synthesis. End on a clean lens called honestly. Source: Episode 2.

Episode 3 — deep-implement · ~12 min · green

Cold open: “green build, broken feature” Live: the per-phase loop, twice

Money shot: the orchestrator spawning a fresh sub-agent per phase — and the two-attempt fix cap producing a blocker report instead of a third try. Decisions: orchestrator-not-author, new-failures-only gating, the contract probe + “green ≠ verified for external shapes.” Source: Episode 3.

Episode 4 — deep-code-review · ~14 min · amber

Cold open: the happy path that doesn't reach the last mile Live: a chain trace, hop by hop Live: --triage as a separate step

Money shot: walking a promised behavior down the canonical chain and finding the hop where it breaks — with evidence at every hop. Decisions: review ≠ triage / findings stay open, the 8-finder fleet, the sev ≥5 verification floor, the model-tier discipline (tell the Haiku story — zero findings on a diff with two known Criticals). This is the flagship episode; give it room. Source: Episode 4.

Episode 5 — The roadmap & the loop · ~10 min · pink

Cross-cutting decisions deep-bug-fix & deep-docs Deep-Learn, end to end

Goal: zoom out. Show the eight cross-cutting decisions as the “grammar” of the series, then preview the two unbuilt skills and trace one bug class through the full Deep-Learn loop (review finds it → card authored → upstream skills prevent it). Source: Bonus episode.

Practical

Filming & scripting tips

SCRIPT FROM THE PAGES

The prose is camera-ready

The panel quotes, the “why this choice” lines, and the callouts are written in spoken cadence. Lift them as narration; don't paraphrase into something flatter.

USE A REAL FEATURE

Dogfood on camera

Run the live segments on one small real feature carried across all four episodes, so viewers see the same effort directory grow stage by stage — the capstone made visible.

NARRATE JUDGMENT

Decisions, not keystrokes

Viewers can't learn “press enter.” They can learn “I'm reaching for breakout here because this tangent would flood my context.” Voice the choice every time.

RECAP THE LOOP

Bookend with the relay

Open and close each episode on the pipeline strip with the current stage lit. By Episode 4, the viewer should feel the loop in their bones.

EVIDENCE ON SCREEN

Show your sources

When you state a rule (“never Haiku, any tier”), flash where it lives. The series is about evidence — model it.

KEEP THE BOUNDARIES SHARP

Show a skill refusing

One of the most memorable shots: ask a skill to cross its boundary (ask deep-plan to write code) and show it declining and pointing to the right sibling. Separation of concerns, dramatized.

Total series runtime

Roughly 64 minutes across six episodes (8 + 11 + 9 + 12 + 14 + 10). Comfortable for a single-sitting course or a weekly drop. Trim the live segments first if you need a tighter cut — the “why” segments are the differentiator and should survive.

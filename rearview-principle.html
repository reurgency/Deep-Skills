The Elevator Pitch

Deep Skills is the distillation of three years of building real applications with AI — a connected series of skills where each phase drives the work forward while continually assessing the previous phase in the rearview mirror.

Plan, review, implement, code-review: every handoff is also an inspection. Errors get caught at the next checkpoint, fixed by the role that owns them, and logged so they never return. By the time you ship, every bug has been seen by the phase that came after it.

You stay in the loop where your judgment matters — and out of the weeds where it doesn't. Quality stops degrading as the work grows. It compounds.

A safety net for every phase before it

The guiding principle is simple: every phase of AI coding is a safety net for the phases that came before. As each phase does its own work, it hunts for errors upstream and calls them out. The goal is zero errors by the time you ship — and the rearview cascade gets there one caught defect at a time.

Every agent stays in its lane

A reviewer that flags a bug doesn't go rogue and rewrite the system. Fixes are performed by the proper role. The implementer implements; the planner re-plans; the reviewer reviews. Clean handoffs, clear ownership — so the correction lands exactly where it belongs and nothing new breaks on the way.

The Inversion

We inverted the curve.

In a single unchecked AI pass, every new feature adds risk faster than value. Quality erodes as the codebase grows — the classic spiral of diminishing returns, where more output quietly buys you more debt.

The Rearview Principle flips it. Because each phase backstops the last, defects are removed faster than they accumulate. Quality compounds as the work moves forward, and the error count bends toward zero right when it matters most — at ship.
